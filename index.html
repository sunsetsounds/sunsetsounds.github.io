Overview

High quality audio usually goes out the window the moment you step outside. But not Lumino. Lumino’s going right out the door with you, sporting a smooth 3D-printed enclosure and sound-reactive LEDs for some flash. Though slightly larger than the average portable speaker, Lumino has the affordable HiFi audio you’ve been waiting for. Lumino is unparalleled when it comes to big sound in a compact form factor. Large drivers and passive radiators give Lumino deep bass response to produce satisfying room-filling sound, and it’s certainly loud enough at a staggering 103dB to tag along when you want to chill at the beach or enjoy a picnic. Powered by high capacity lithium-ion batteries, Lumino is capable of playing music all-day long and can get hours of playtime from minutes of charging. Its modern aesthetic is made possible by multiple layers of post-processing, and the hand assembly of internal components requires even more attention from our talented team. As a representation of our extensive research in sound reproduction and speaker transducer design, Sunset Sounds has engineered a product that can produce HiFi audio wherever your day takes you.

Legacy Models

This is a short sample of some of the previous models that we've created that did not live up to expectations, were discontinued, or remained as prototypes. A look into the history of Sunset Sounds' speakers and the persistent trial and error we endure in our pursuit of bringing premium sound quality to portable speakers.

LBHF

Built in Spring 2020, LBHF was created to be a one off design for a birthday, but we soon realized its full capability. Originally called Low Budget HiFi due to its intention to produce HiFi sound quality at a lower price point and portable form factor, LBHF was a name that we eventually became affectionately attached to. One of the most portable designs we had built at this point, LBHF clocked in at just under 5 pounds, a major achievement at the time. Still being fully 3D-printed, we took our lessons from Horizon and brought new features and revised others. Digital Signal Processing (DSP) in its software form made its grand appearance in this design, allowing us to more effectively design our audio architecture. In addition, this was the first design to be simulated in CAD to optimize use of its twin passive radiators. LBHF plays an important role in Sunset Sounds' production history, being the Proof of Concept (POC) that would give rise to Lumino/Lumino+ in the summer of 2020.

Horizon

Hailing in the Summer 2019, this speaker was designed as a portable smart speaker with a voice assistant and many features. With sound quality being more of a concern in this design, this was a speaker that was built during a transitional period in Sunset Sounds when we decided to focus on bringing HiFi to portable speakers. Being smaller and lighter than Signature at around 14 inches tall and weighing around 8 pounds, Horizon proved to be an improvement in portability and convenience, but at the cost of complexity with bass boost circuits created by hand using NE5532 pre-amps and a smart home receiver that had Alexa or Google Home functionality. Being the first speaker we created utilizing 3D printing and digital signal processing (DSP), Horizon serves as a hallmark of learning experience as we learned how to better design and engineer audio systems and electrical subsystems that would improve user interaction and listening experience.

Signature and Signature SE

Created in the Summer 2018, this model was the first speaker ever created by Sunset Sounds. It falls far from the speakers we make today, but it still is a force to be reckoned with as a party speaker. Featuring four 6.5-inch drivers in an unconventional isobaric loading configuration to achieve bass in smaller enclosures, sound-reactive RGB LEDs, and IoT connectivity for remote turn-on with voice assistants. Signature was certainly a unique speaker in appearance and functionality, but it was not a viable design due to its extremely high cost and weight at a whopping 15 pounds.

About Us

Sunset Sounds started as a project in 2017 by Derrick and Jared when they were seniors in high school. Since then, Sunset Sounds has evolved into a fully-fledged small business with multiple prototypes, grants, achievements, and most importantly: passionate and hardworking individuals that make a successful team.

Open Positions and Applications

Product Engineer - EB102

Functions as a product manager and systems engineer with strong interdisciplinary skills in applied business and engineering. The product engineer considers all aspects and parameters surrounding a product and optimizes its effectiveness and efficiency. Highly driven individual skill and strong leadership are important for the Product Engineer to achieve goals, foster product development, and promote business growth. Engineering, applied business, and leadership experience highly preferred. CAD, prototyping, and audio system knowledge preferred but not required.

Business Development Associate - BO102

An integral part of growth, the Business Development Associate preforms essential tasks in networking and communications. Whether its discussing engineering challenges or confirming sales, they set up meetings and are critical in sales negotiations. Developing relationships to promote team morale, make important sales (B2B and B2C), and working closely with founders are basic responsibilities. Sales or negotiations experience is preferred.

Sales Development and Sales Engineer - BO201

Sales is an important part of any business. Sales Development Reps are responsible for acquiring clients, researching, and executing transactions. Understanding of the product, market, and audience are important. Technical expertise and people skills are also critical in effectively describing product questions and inquiries. Sales and technical experience preferred.

Electrical/CS Subsystem Engineer - EE201

Works to develop existing EECS subsystems into more efficient, optimized solutions. Designs integrated circuits and PCBs to be fabricated. Knowledge in audio systems, BMS, amplifiers, crossover networks, advanced time-domain phase shifts, and DSP algorithims are preferred but not required. Experience with Atmega chipsets, Arduinos, and Op-Amps highly preferred.

Project Assistant - BA303

Works closely with high level processes involving product operations and organization of sprints and goals. Management and organizational skills are needed to facilitate prototyping schedules and product deliverables on a timeline. Scrum or Kanban experience is preferred.
